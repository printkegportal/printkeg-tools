What is a vector SVG file?

SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics) stores images as mathematical paths rather than pixels. Unlike JPG or PNG files, an SVG can be scaled to any size — from a business card to a billboard — without losing sharpness or clarity. It's the standard format for logos, icons, and print-ready artwork.

What converts best?

  • Logos and wordmarks
  • Icons and simple illustrations
  • Flat or limited-color artwork
  • Line art and sketches
  • Clipart and graphic elements

Complex photographs will be stylized and interpreted — not photorealistic.

How to use this tool

Upload a JPG or PNG, choose a detail level, and click Convert. The AI analyzes your image and generates a true SVG using only vector paths, shapes, and gradients — no embedded pixels. Download the result and open it in Illustrator, Inkscape, Figma, or any vector editor.

Detail level explained

Simple — Bold, clean shapes. Best for logos and icons you plan to edit further.

Balanced — A mix of accuracy and clean output. Good all-around starting point.

Detailed — Maximum complexity with gradients and fine paths. Best for illustrations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this vector converter really free?
Yes — completely free. No account, no watermarks, no usage limits. It's one of several free tools Printkeg provides for artists and designers.
Can I use the SVG for commercial printing?
Yes. SVG files are resolution-independent, making them ideal for any print size. You can also open the file in Adobe Illustrator or Inkscape to refine it before sending to a print shop.
What's the difference between this and tools like Vectorizer.ai or Adobe Express?
Most vectorizers use edge-detection algorithms to trace pixels into paths. This tool uses AI vision to understand and interpret the image, which can produce more stylistically clean results — especially for logos and illustrations.
Is my image stored or saved anywhere?
No. Your image is sent directly to the AI model for conversion and is not stored on our servers. The output SVG exists only in your browser session.
What file size should I use?
Images under 4MB work best. Very large files may slow processing. For best results, crop your image to just the subject before uploading.
